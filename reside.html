The Design Process for reSide

The Short story

Life as a renter, especially when experiencing issues in their home can leave a person feeling stressed and disillusioned. I set out to create an accessible & affordable solution for renters. Knowing that you’re getting 100% accurate legal advice, affordably can really help a renter that’s having problems with their landlord.

Jump to final product

Why can renting turn into such a legally perilous journey?

Knowing when and how to address issues with your landlord or management company can be stressful and potentially expensive.

Initially

I set out to create an app for renters to access expert advice and wisdom from professionals and other legal resources.

These assumption served as a starting point for my research.

Not only to test the validity of my assumptions but to get a better understanding of the market and to refine my project, I conducted competitive analysis, and conducted 5 users interviews. Doing this research helped me identify gaps among existing legal advice-giving apps, it also helped me understand my users’ needs and pain points.

  • face obstacles knowing what their rights are

    Putting it to the test

    To assess reSides’s usability for new users interacting with the web app for the first time on mobile, I gathered a diverse group of 6 participants who are currently renters or were renting in the recent past (aged 30- 62) for moderated remote usability tests. These tests prompted me to make the following 3 major changes on my final prototype:

    View full usability testing

    1. Users were confused about the top nav bar icons

    The icons were simplified into text and then the top nav bar was further refined.

    2. Users said they were less likely to finish singing up for a free trial where they had to

    The payment method requirement was dropped and account creation was moved to after users write their message to a lawyer

    3. Users said they would rather self-serve than chat with a lawyer

    While there was always self-serve options we will now work to make these sections more robust and post launch we’ll focus our efforts to see how we can create the content our users want and need.
